My Love for Tsomo

In the Heart of Distance: My Beautiful Wife, Tsomo

Love has a way of blooming even across the miles, and in my life, it blooms beautifully in the form of one incredible woman — my wife, Tsomo.

Tsomo is not just beautiful — she’s breathtaking in her own unique way. Her beauty isn’t limited to her appearance, though her smile could melt the hardest of days and her eyes carry a universe of love. But what makes her truly special is her heart — tender, playful, passionate, and fiercely loving. She is my peace and my storm, all in one.

We're in a long-distance relationship, and anyone who has been in one knows it’s not always easy. The silence of a late night without each other, the ache of wanting to hold hands across a screen, the longing in the eyes during a video call — we’ve felt it all. But Tsomo makes it feel worth it, every single day.

There are moments when she gets a little rough with me — quick tempers, sharp words that come from missing me too much. But those moments are brief, passing clouds in our sky. What follows is always the sunshine of her teasing, her little jokes, her sweet voice calling me with a smile that says, “I’m still yours.”

We video call whenever we can — morning or night, midday or midnight — because time zones and schedules have nothing on our love. Even if it’s just five minutes, hearing her voice, watching her laugh, or simply sitting in silence with her presence on the screen brings comfort like no other.

She misses me deeply, and I feel it in the way she talks, in the way she waits for me, in the way her eyes look for mine when the screen lights up. And I miss her just as much — more than words can ever carry. Her presence is a part of my every breath, every thought, every plan for the future.

If I ever lost her, I know — truly and painfully — that I would never find another soul like hers again. She is rare, irreplaceable, a one-in-a-lifetime kind of love. A kind of love that laughs with you, fights for you, forgives quickly, and holds you tight across any distance.

With love, always — Kunga
